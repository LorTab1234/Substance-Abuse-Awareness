About us

Substance Abuse Awareness (SAA) is a not-for-profit organization that specialises in spreading awareness about Substance Abuse to the people of Western Sydney. Since 2024, we have been dedicated to helping those who have been affected by Substance Abuse and teach about the negative effects of Substance Abuse.

Our Mission

Our Mission is to help those that have been affected by substance abuse either directly or indirectly. We do this through our many initiatives and services that we provide to those in most need of our assistance. We also provide lessons to younger generations, especially students, about the negative effects of Substance Abuse.

What is Substance Abuse

Substance Abuse is the overuse of drugs such as both medicinal and recreational drugs, alcohol, tabacco or any sort of substance that provides stimulation or medical benefits at a dangerous level. This usually involves an individual misusing the substance in a way that can lead to physical, psychological, social and/or mental harm to the individual.

What are the consequences of Substance Abuse?

Substance Abuse can have different effects for different people as people have various reactions to misusing and abusing substances. However, there are a multitude of both short and long term effects that may impact your physical body, mental health and social life negatively.

Physical consequences of substance abuse may include:

  • Damage to Organs such as your heart, lungs, and liver
  • Cardiovascular problems including increased heart rate and high blood pressure
  • Respiratory problems
  • Hormonal imbalance
  • Weakened Immune System
  • Injuries and Accidents as a result of using drugs and alcohol

    • Mental consequences of substance abuse may include:

  • Addiction and dependency on substances (Substance Use Disorder or SUD)
  • Mood Disorders such as depression and anxiety
  • Psychosis which can cause hallucinations, delusions and paranoia
  • Cognitive Impairment affecting decision making, attention and memory
  • Increased risk of Suicide

    • If you are experiencing these things, please go to our initiatives and services page to get the assistance you need.

    What are the factors that may cause Substance Abuse?

    There are multiple factors that may cause or lead to abusing substances. It depends on the case but generally, substance abuse is usually caused either due to mental stress/trauma, the addictive nature of the drug they are using, or because they were forced to or just did it for the sake of doing it.

    The most common cause for Substance abuse however is as a result of mental issues such as depression, post-traumatic stress disorder or (PTSD), attention deficit disorder etc. Substance abuse is a way for a temporary escape from this reality as regularly using these substances can lead to the effects of their mental illness to be numbed.

    This can therefore lead to people relying on drugs and alcohol to relieve the mental and/or physical pain they are feeling despite knowing the disadvantages abusing drugs may cause. That is why it is imperative to never use drugs and alcohol as the only answer to relieve any mental and/or physical stress.

    Alternative ways such as outreaching for help, participating in Substance Abuse Awareness initiatives and events, venting any frustrations and experiences to therapists are safer and more effective ways to potentially mentally and physically heal individuals.
